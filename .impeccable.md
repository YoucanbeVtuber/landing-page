## Design Context

### Users
Live2D illustrators, riggers, and VTuber creators — **global audience, primarily Japan and USA, with Korea included**. They spend hours manually separating character illustration layers before rigging. Creative professionals and hobbyists who care deeply about their characters. They work at a desk, often late at night, surrounded by art tools. The visual language should feel at home for JP/US creators familiar with tools like Clip Studio, Photoshop, and Live2D Cubism.

### Brand Personality
**Creative · Playful · Fancy** (창의·경쾌·팬시)

Voice: warm, knowledgeable, a little delightful. Like a skilled artisan who happens to have AI superpowers. Not corporate, not cold. Think: the inside of a well-lit illustration studio — warm lamp light, quality paper, a sense that someone cared about every detail.

Emotional goal: the user should feel *confident* handing over their precious character art, and *delighted* by how easy the process is.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Light. Creamy warm off-white, not stark white. Feels like quality paper or a canvas, not a dashboard.
- **Color palette**: OKLCH warm off-white background. Warm mauve-violet accent (oklch ~55% 0.19 315) — distinct from generic indigo tech palettes. Tinted neutrals that lean toward the brand hue.
- **Typography**: Gloock (editorial serif display) + Hanken Grotesk (clean UI/body). The serif gives "fancy" and signals this is for people who care about craft. Works well for EN/JP/KR mixed content contexts.
- **Dropzone**: Large, bold, central. The upload area IS the hero. Not buried below a wall of text. Make it feel inviting and obvious.
- **Anti-references**: No glassmorphism glow cards. No gradient text. No side-stripe borders. No generic purple-to-blue tech gradient. No "AI startup" look.

### Design Principles
1. **Canvas over dashboard** — Every surface should feel like a creative workspace, not a SaaS product.
2. **Upload is the hero** — The dropzone should dominate the first viewport. Everything else supports it.
3. **Warmth through color** — Never pure white or pure black. Always tinted toward the brand mauve-warm hue.
4. **Editorial typography** — Use the serif display font boldly. It signals craft and quality.
5. **Reduce to essentials** — No decorative icons on every heading. No redundant labels. Let space do the work.
